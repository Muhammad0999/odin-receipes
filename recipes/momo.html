🥟 Steamed Momos Recipe

Description

Momos are a popular type of South Asian dumpling, originating from Tibet and Nepal, that have become a beloved staple street food across India, Bhutan, and beyond. They consist of a savory filling—traditionally minced meat (like yak or chicken) or vegetables—wrapped in a thin, dough-based wrapper and typically steamed, though they are also popular fried or pan-fried.

📦 Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 300g minced chicken or pork (or cabbage for veg)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp spring onion / coriander (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp black pepper powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp salt (for filling)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

📋 Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and oil. Gradually add warm water and knead into a smooth, firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, combine minced meat, onion, garlic, ginger, green chili, spring onion/coriander, soy sauce, black pepper, cumin powder, salt, and sesame oil. Mix everything well until fully combined. Keep aside.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle (about 3–4 inches diameter) using a rolling pin. The edges should be thinner than the center.
  4. Place 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of each wrapper. Fold and pleat the edges together, pinching and twisting to seal tightly. Make sure there are no gaps.
  5. Grease the steamer basket with oil to prevent sticking. Bring water to a boil in the steamer pot. Place momos in the basket leaving some space between each.
  6. Steam the momos on medium-high heat for 15–20 minutes. They are ready when the wrapper turns slightly translucent and filling is cooked through.
  7. Remove momos carefully from the steamer. Serve hot with spicy tomato-sesame chutney or schezwan sauce on the side. Enjoy!
